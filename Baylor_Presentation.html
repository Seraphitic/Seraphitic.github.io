Roots/Zeros of Polynomials

Make sure that everyone is on board with the definition of a root or a zero. Perhaps make sure the connection is there that this is what they are solving for when using the quadratic formula

A root or a zero of a polynomial is just the \(x\) value where it intersects the x-axis (i.e. where \(y=0\)).

Fundamental Theorem of Algebra

Make sure it is clear there can be less than \(n\) roots Use an example of a quadratic having no x-intercept

"A polynomial of degree \(n\) can have, at most, \(n\) distinct, real roots."

Odd Degree Polynomials

Point out that this is a consequence of the end behavior Since one end will go up and the other will go down, at least one of them must, eventually, hit the x-axis.

"A polynomial of odd degree will have at least one real root."

Turning Points

"A polynomial of degree \(n\) can have, at most, \(n-1\) turning points."
